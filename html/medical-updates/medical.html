A Guide to Annual Medical Updates

Every year, every Scouting family must update their AMHR. What does that mean? Here's a helpful video from Scouting America: Annual Health and Medical Record Safety Moment

Parts A and B of AMHR are required for all who participate in outdoor activities with Troop 2338. Parts A, B, and C, filled out by the family and a physician, are required for all Scouts and strongly encouraged for all Scouters attending Summer Camp or any outing that lasts more than 72 hours. For high adventure camps, medical advisories may be required to show physicians, depending. This page has more info..

What should I turn in?

When you register with Troop 2338, we give you a packet of paperwork. In it is a medical packet with forms A and B. This is due back with a copy of your health insurance as soon as possible. Turn in one for each Scout and participating adult.

About a month before Summer Camp, schedule an appointment for a Camp Physical. It may be with your family physician or with a walk-in clinic - many places offer them. Take the Camp Physical Form with you. Fill out parts A and B, then ask the physician to fill out part C. 

Before leaving to go to camp, turn in copies of:

  • Medical Forms A, B, and C
  • Proof of Insurance

Troop 2338 requests that you keep your originals. Adult leaders can help make copies if needed.

What does Troop 2338 do with your information?

Troop 2338 keeps one paper copy of your information under lock and key, for use by Scoutmasters and adult leaders during camp checkin and in case of emergencies. Troop 2338 does not keep your medical information online in cloud storage, databases, or in any other public system, and we will not ask for you to send it by email or text. Occasionally, a camp may ask you to upload a digitized copy for each camper in your household. We will assist with that securely, if you request.

What about medication?

Regular or temporary medication may be needed on an outing. A parent may request that adults administer the medication. Please see the handbook for more information and FAQs. If you would like to request medication administration by an adult leader, please fill out a Medication Administration Request Form and give it to the Scoutmaster one week before camp.

Questions?

Don't hesitate to ask about anything that confuses you. Any troop leader can help you by email, in person, or by text. Or, use our contact form..
