Facts about Troop 2338

  • We are an all boy troop for ages 11–17. Adult members are welcome and encouraged.
  • We believe that an Eagle rank is possible, and we will help every interested young person to reach that goal.
  • We enforce the Principles of Scouting for all youth, parents, and adult members of Troop 2338.
  • We are small and agile, with a high adult-to-youth ratio.
  • We do not discriminate based on race, religion, wealth, or national origin. All are welcome.

Basic Concepts of Scouting

Scout Oath: On my honor I will do my best to do my duty to God and my country and to obey the Scout Law; to help other people at all times; to keep myself physically strong, mentally awake, and morally straight.

Scout Law: A Scout is trustworthy, loyal, helpful, friendly, courteous, kind, obedient, cheerful, thrifty, brave, clean, and reverent.

Scout Mission: The mission of Scouting America is to prepare young people to make ethical and moral choices over their lifetimes by instilling in them the values of the Scout Oath and Scout Law.

In Troop 2338, we value...

Character Development

Building strong personal qualities, values, and outlook.

Learning confidence, honesty, and self-respect.

Showing respect for others, regardless of differences.

Living by one’s own religious beliefs.

Citizenship Training

Working together under troop rules that serve the common good.

Taking pride in national heritage.

Understanding social, economic, and governmental systems.

Practicing service, tolerance, and community involvement.

Mental and Physical Fitness

Strengthening the body through exercise and outdoor activities.

Developing healthy habits.

Avoiding drugs, alcohol, and tobacco.

Building sound judgment, resourcefulness, and decision-making skills.
